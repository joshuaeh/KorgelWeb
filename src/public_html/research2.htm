The research program focuses on the development of new chemical synthetic and processing routes to nanomaterials that have potential industrial importance.

For example, we have been developing solution-based synthetic routes that enable large quantities of semiconductor nanowires to be produced. Currently, hundreds of milligrams of crystalline nanowires can be produced in a single reaction. These nanowires are dispersible in solvents for low temperature deposition on a variety of substrates, including plastics. Nanowires have been proposed as the basis for many different technologies, including transistors, chemical and optical sensors, batteries, solar cells, superhydrophobic coatings, polymer composites, thermoelectrics and nanoelectromechanical devices. There are many questions about the behavior of nanowires and therefore we study their properties and build prototype devices implementing these materials as well.
